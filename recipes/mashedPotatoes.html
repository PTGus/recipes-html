Mashed Potatoes

Stock Image from iStock

About This Recipe

These classic mashed potatoes turn simple ingredients into pure comfort—silky, fluffy, and rich without ever feeling heavy. Using humble russets, warm milk, and plenty of butter, this method brings out their natural creaminess while keeping every bite smooth and lump-free. By boiling the potatoes gently, drying them briefly, and mashing them while still steaming, you get a texture that's irresistibly light and beautifully buttery.

Beloved for its reliability and straightforward technique, this recipe has become a staple for holiday tables and everyday dinners alike. Whether served as a cozy weeknight side or as the base for your favorite add-ins—garlic, herbs, cheese, or anything else—it delivers that comforting, nostalgic flavor that makes mashed potatoes feel like home.

Ingredients

  • 3 large russet potatoes, peeled and halved lengthwise
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 1/4 cup butter
  • salt and ground black pepper to taste

Cooking Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. Place potatoes in a large pot and cover with cold salted water by about 1 inch. Bring to a boil. Reduce heat to medium-low, cover and simmer until tender and easily pierced with a fork, 20 to 25 minutes.
  3. Drain potatoes, then return to the pot. Turn heat to high and allow potatoes to dry for about 30 seconds. Turn off heat.
  4. Mash potatoes with a potato masher twice around the pot, then mash in milk and butter until smooth and fluffy.
  5. Season with salt and black; whisk until evenly distributed, about 15 seconds.
  6. Serve and enjoy!
